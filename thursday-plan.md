## Airport → Apartment

**Take the CAT (City Airport Train)**, not the S7 regional train. The CAT is the roomier, traveler-oriented option:

- 16 minutes nonstop from VIE to Wien Mitte
- Runs every 30 minutes
- Wide aisles, dedicated luggage racks at the end of each car, level boarding from the platform
- ~€12 per person at the machine (cheaper if bought online in advance)

The S7 is half the price but slower (25–35 min), more crowded, designed for commuters not for travelers with suitcases. **Skip it.**

Wien Mitte to apartment: 5–7 min walk with wheeled luggage. Streets are flat.

## Morning: Café Schwarzenberg

**Address**: Kärntner Ring 17, on Schwarzenbergplatz, 1010 Vienna
**Walk from Wien Mitte**: ~12–15 min slowly down Landstraßer Hauptstraße and across Schwarzenbergplatz. Flat, paved, fine for wheeled luggage.

Why here: synthesis-validated. Barrier-free, predictable seating, classic Viennese coffeehouse atmosphere (founded 1861) — marble tables, Thonet chairs, wood newspaper rack on the wall, opens early. Spacious enough that two suitcases tuck under the table or beside the chairs without drama.

**Order**: *Wiener Frühstück* — a kaisersemmel roll, butter, ham, jam, soft-boiled egg in a cup, plus a *Melange* (Vienna's milky coffee, similar to a flat white). Linger over a second Melange. This is Viennese coffeehouse culture — sitting for an hour or two over one breakfast is the actual point.

Then walk back to the apartment via Rennweg (~8–10 min, flat).

## Afternoon: Innere Stadt walk

After the nap, **U3 from Wien Mitte to Stephansplatz** — 3-minute ride, both stations have elevators.

**The loop:**
1. Step out at Stephansplatz — **Stephansdom** is right there. Free entry. Sit on a pew for 10 minutes. Look up at the Gothic vault and the multicolored tile roof from outside.
2. Walk slowly down **Graben** — the wide pedestrian boulevard with the baroque Pestsäule (Plague Column) in the middle.
3. Continue onto **Kohlmarkt** — Vienna's elegant shopping street, where Demel and other Habsburg-era storefronts are. Not for shopping, for the facades.
4. **Café pause** — pick one:
   - **Café Diglas** (Wollzeile 10), 3 min from cathedral. Classic, large, marble tables, excellent Apfelstrudel.
   - **Café Frauenhuber** (Himmelpfortgasse 6), 5 min from Stephansplatz. Claims to be Vienna's oldest café (1824); Mozart and Beethoven both performed in this building. Smaller, atmospheric.
5. **Back to Stephansplatz**, U3 to Wien Mitte (~3 min underground).
6. **Billa Plus** stop on the way back to the apartment for light dinner: cold cuts, cheese, bread, salad, soup, fruit, water. Nothing heavy.

## Practical bits

- **Buy a 24-hour Vienna transit ticket** (€8) at any vending machine when you first arrive at Wien Mitte. Covers all U-Bahn, trams, and buses for 24 hours from validation. Pays for itself across the airport-to-apartment leg, the Stephansplatz round trip, and any Friday morning transit.
- **Father's evening at the apartment**: make sure he has dinner sorted from the Billa stop, water, wifi password if needed, before you leave for the concert.
- **Backup if weather is bad**: skip the Innere Stadt walk; instead spend the afternoon at Café Schwarzenberg again, or at Café Prückel opposite Stadtpark (5 min from the apartment). Same calm shape, fully indoors.
