09:30 – 10:00
CAT (City Airport Train) → Wien Mitte
Why CAT, not S7

Take the CAT, not the S7. CAT is the roomier, traveller-oriented option:

  • 16 minutes nonstop from VIE to Wien Mitte
  • Runs every 30 minutes
  • Wide aisles, dedicated luggage racks at the end of each car, level boarding
  • ~€14 per person at the machine (cheaper online in advance)

The S7 is half the price but slower (25–35 min), more crowded, designed for commuters not travellers with suitcases. Skip it.

Buy a 24-hour Vienna transit ticket (€8) at any vending machine when you first arrive at Wien Mitte. Covers all U-Bahn, trams, buses for 24 h from validation.

Step-by-step: how to get on the CAT and pay for 3 tickets

Vienna International Airport (VIE / Schwechat) → Apartment (Blütengasse 14–16, 1030 Wien) via the City Airport Train. For 3 people (Ivan + two parents), Ivan paying.

  1. Plane lands at VIE

    Bulgaria has been in Schengen since 2025, so no passport control on arrival from a Schengen flight — walk straight off the plane and through.

  2. Baggage claim

    Head down to baggage claim, find your suitcases. Allow 15–20 minutes for bags, especially if multiple flights land at once.

  3. Walk through customs

    Take the green "Nothing to Declare" lane — just keep walking, no stop — into the arrivals hall (Ankunftshalle).

  4. Find the green CAT signs

    Bright green "CAT" or "City Airport Train" signs are visible from the arrivals hall. They point to escalators and elevators going down to the train platforms. The CAT has its own dedicated platform; the branding is unmistakable.

  5. Buy three tickets at a CAT vending machine

    There are CAT-branded machines (green and black) in the arrivals hall as well as down at the platform. Use the one in the arrivals hall before heading down.

    • Touch the screen to wake it up
    • Tap the UK/US flag for English
    • Choose "Single ticket" (one-way)
    • Choose route: Vienna Airport → Wien Mitte
    • Set quantity to 3 using the +/– buttons or quantity field
    • Total: ~€42 (€14 × 3 at the machine)
    • Pay with a credit card — easier than fumbling cash with three tickets to buy. Tap-to-pay or chip-and-PIN both work; Visa/Mastercard/Amex all accepted.
    • Three paper tickets print out. Keep all three together in one pocket.
    Save ~€6 total: buy online at cityairporttrain.com before travelling — 3 tickets cost about €36, and you'll get QR codes on your phone instead of paper. Either works equally well.

  6. Down to the platform

    Escalator or elevator. Use the elevator with luggage and parents.

  7. Identify the right train

    CAT trains are unmistakable — bright green and white, with "City Airport Train" written along the side. They look obviously different from the regular ÖBB and S-Bahn trains on adjacent tracks.

    • If you see a train that's red-and-white (ÖBB Railjet) or red-and-grey (S-Bahn), that's NOT your train — wait for the green one.
    • The platform display shows the next CAT departure time.

  8. CAT departures every 30 minutes

    If you just missed one, the wait isn't long. Your ticket is valid for 4 hours from purchase, so missing one is not a problem.

  9. Board

    Level boarding (no step up from the platform — wheel your suitcase straight on). Wide aisles, generous seats, dedicated luggage racks at the ends of each car. Settle into any unreserved seat.

  10. Ride — 16 minutes nonstop

    Announcements in German and English: "Nächster Halt: Wien Mitte. Next stop: Wien Mitte."

    A conductor may walk through to check tickets mid-trip — just show your three paper tickets (or QR codes). They don't need to be validated/stamped separately; the date is already printed on them.

  11. Arrive at Wien Mitte (final stop)

    Everyone gets off. Wien Mitte is a busy multi-level station with shops and several transit lines.

    Follow exit signs marked "Ausgang Landstraßer Hauptstraße" — that's the direction toward the apartment. Take the elevator or escalator up to the surface.

  12. Wien Mitte → Apartment

    Blütengasse 14–16 is about 5–7 minutes' walk. Up Landstraßer Hauptstraße or its parallel side streets. Wheeled-luggage-friendly the whole way.

Practical notes

  • The ticket itself: a paper card about credit-card sized, with the date and route printed on it. No special validation needed.
  • If you accidentally get on the wrong train: the ÖBB Railjet also goes from VIE to Wien Mitte in 16 min, but your CAT ticket is not valid on it. If a conductor catches you on the wrong train, explain politely and you'll likely just need to buy an ÖBB ticket on board (~€5 extra per person). Easy mistake to avoid: trust the green-and-white branding.
  • The platform might be slightly cold (it's below ground level). Have your jackets handy.
  • Children under 14 ride free with a paying adult — not relevant for this trip but useful for future reference.

Café Schwarzenberg — what to order, why here

Address: Kärntner Ring 17, on Schwarzenbergplatz, 1010 Wien · map

Why here: classic Viennese coffeehouse (founded 1861) — marble tables, Thonet chairs, wood newspaper rack, opens early. Spacious enough that two suitcases tuck under the table without drama. Barrier-free, predictable seating.

Order: Wiener Frühstück — a kaisersemmel roll, butter, ham, jam, soft-boiled egg in a cup, plus a Melange (Vienna's milky coffee, similar to a flat white). Linger over a second Melange. Sitting for an hour or two over one breakfast is the actual point.

The loop, step by step

U3 from Wien Mitte to Stephansplatz — 3 min ride, both stations have elevators.

  • Stephansdom — free entry. Sit on a pew for 10 minutes. Look up at the Gothic vault and the multicoloured tile roof from outside.
  • Graben — wide pedestrian boulevard, baroque Pestsäule (Plague Column) in the middle.
  • Kohlmarkt — Vienna's elegant shopping street, Habsburg-era storefronts. Not for shopping, for the façades.
  • Café pause — pick one:
    • Café Diglas (Wollzeile 10), 3 min from cathedral. Classic, large, marble tables, excellent Apfelstrudel. map
    • Café Frauenhuber (Himmelpfortgasse 6), 5 min from Stephansplatz. Vienna's oldest café (1824); Mozart and Beethoven both performed in this building. map
  • U3 back to Wien Mitte (~3 min).
Bad-weather backup: skip the Innere Stadt walk; spend the afternoon at Café Schwarzenberg again, or at Café Prückel opposite Stadtpark (5 min from the apartment). Same calm shape, fully indoors.

09:15 – 11:15
Upper Belvedere — Klimt rooms, Marble Hall, Schiele
What to see, in what order

Three levels with elevator: ground, Beletage (1st floor), 2nd floor. Start with the Beletage while energy is fresh — the headline rooms are there.

Must-see sequence on the Beletage:

  • Marble Hall — grand central reception room, frescoed ceiling, postcard view down the gardens to St. Stephen's spire. Also where the Austrian State Treaty was signed in 1955.
  • The Klimt rooms:
    • The Kiss (1907–1908) — gold-leaf icon. Smaller than expected; tends to draw a crowd. Just stand and look.
    • Judith I (1901) — Klimt's other gold masterpiece. More intense than The Kiss. Worth a long pause.
  • Schiele rooms — Klimt's protégé. Look for Death and the Maiden and The Family.
  • Vienna 1900 / Secession material — Kokoschka, Moser, the Klimt circle.

Then ground floor for medieval and baroque Austrian art, second floor for 19th-century Austrian art (Biedermeier, Hans Makart) — skip these freely if mom is flagging. Klimt and the Marble Hall are the trip-justifying pieces.

Cloakroom near the entrance · benches in galleries · small café inside Upper Belvedere for a sit-down break · audio guides available (no Bulgarian — Ivan provides context as you go).

Garden walkthrough → Botanical Garden

After Upper Belvedere, exit out the back onto the upper terrace. Pause for the postcard view down the gardens. Walk down the formal terraces — fountains, sphinxes, statuary. Stairs at points but ramped paths alongside.

About halfway down (or near the Lower Belvedere level), head east toward the Alpengarten. From the Alpengarten there's a connecting gate into the Botanical Garden of the University of Vienna.

Confirm the gate is open at the Upper Belvedere ticket desk when you arrive (it usually is during open hours). If the gate is closed: exit on Rennweg, walk ~3 min east to the Botanical Garden's main entrance at Mechelgasse 2 · map
11:30 – 12:15
Botanical Garden of the University of Vienna
What's blooming, how to wander

Free entry, no ticket. Open from 10:00 to dusk. Flat, paved paths, plenty of benches.

In early May, look for: late tulips, magnolias (possibly past peak), peonies starting, alpine wildflowers, mature trees in fresh leaf, Japanese cherry varieties if any are still blooming.

Don't try to "do it all" — pick a slow loop, sit a few times, leave when satisfied. Exit on Rennweg for tram 71 to lunch.

12:45 / 13:00
Lunch — Wiener Schnitzel
Primary & backup · what to order

Primary: Meissl & Schadn — Schubertring 10–12, 1010 Wien (in the Grand Ferdinand Hotel) · map

Open daily 11:30–22:00. Book via Quandoo (app or quandoo.at) — real-time availability, can book same-day. Their own site (meisslundschadn.at) also has online booking. Ideal: book Thursday evening. Acceptable: book Friday morning from the Botanical Garden.

Why: destination Wiener Schnitzel restaurant. Veal (real Wiener Schnitzel is only veal). Open salon kitchen — watch the Schnitzel pounded and fried tableside. Refined but not stuffy. Michelin Bib Gourmand. Calm enough for an elderly diner.

Order: Frittatensuppe to start (clear beef broth with sliced pancake-strips), Wiener Schnitzel with Erdäpfel-Vogerlsalat (potato-and-lamb's-lettuce salad), Kaiserschmarrn with stewed plums to finish, glass of Grüner Veltliner.

Backup: Plachuttas Gasthaus zur Oper — Walfischgasse 5/7, 1010 Wien (right by Vienna State Opera) · map · plachutta-oper.at

Vienna's most popular Wiener Schnitzel three years running. Veal, classical preparation. Traditional Gasthaus atmosphere. Equivalent quality tier. Use this if Meissl & Schadn is full on May 1, or if mom prefers a more traditional inn atmosphere.

~14:30
Afternoon — pick A or B on the day
Option A: Karlskirche · Option B: Stadtpark + Kursalon

Option A — Karlskirche + Resselpark · ~45 min · lighter

Karlskirche is a 5-min walk south of Meissl & Schadn (or 8 min from Plachuttas). Vienna's other postcard baroque dome — giant green copper dome, two enormous Roman-style spiral columns flanking the entrance.

Step inside. Just pay basic entry (skip the panoramic lift inside the dome — too much for tired legs), sit on a pew, look up at the dome from below. Then sit at the Resselpark reflecting pool in front for fifteen minutes. map

Adds Vienna's other major baroque sight at almost zero mobility cost.

Option B — Stadtpark + Kursalon · ~75 min · slightly more

Tram from Karlsplatz/Oper to Stadtpark, walk slowly through the park, find the gold Johann Strauss statue in the gilded archway — Vienna's most photographed statue. Sit at the Kursalon café terrace for a Melange and a slice of Sachertorte. The Kursalon is where Strauss conducted On the Beautiful Blue Danube premieres in the 1860s — a poetic close to the trip's musical arc with Thursday's symphony concert. map

Then ~5 min walk home through the park.

How to choose: A if mom is tired or weather is poor (Karlskirche is indoors). B if she's energised and wants the concert-connection moment in fresh air.

Afternoon
Hofburg "imperial double" — pick by energy
Sisi + Apartments · Schatzkammer · or both

The Hofburg complex is the Habsburgs' city palace and the seat of imperial power for over 600 years. Two ticketed pieces; separate tickets. map

Sisi Museum + Imperial Apartments + Silver Collection · ~90 min

The "Habsburgs at home" experience that Belvedere doesn't give you.

  • Sisi Museum — Empress Elisabeth's life: unhappy royal marriage, beauty rituals, exercise obsession, wandering, assassination by an anarchist in Geneva 1898.
  • Imperial Apartments — 19 rooms where Franz Joseph and Sisi actually lived: bedrooms, audience chamber, his austere iron camp-bed (he slept on it for 50 years), her exercise room, dining table set for state guests.
  • Silver Collection — imperial tableware, including a 30-meter-long baroque silver centerpiece for state banquets, gold-plated services for 140 guests.

Pre-book timed entry on sisimuseum-hofburg.at. Saturday is a normal opening day (not the May 1 issue).

Imperial Treasury (Schatzkammer) · ~45 min

One of the great treasure rooms of Europe. Separate ticket from Sisi/Apartments, different wing of the Hofburg.

  • Crown of the Holy Roman Empire (10th century, oldest crown in Europe)
  • Imperial regalia — orb, sceptre, ceremonial sword
  • Crown of the Austrian Empire
  • The Burgundian inheritance — treasures from the 1477 marriage of Maximilian I to Mary of Burgundy
  • One of the world's largest emeralds — a baroque perfume jar, 2,860 carats, carved from a single emerald
  • Habsburg jewels and personal collection
  • Religious relics and the "narwhal horn" kept as a unicorn-horn relic — one of the strangest items in any treasury

If mom is a "this craftsmanship is unbelievable" person, this is unmissable. If she's more focused on the human/imperial-life side, the Sisi/Apartments piece is the priority.

Just Sisi + Apartments (~90 min + coffeehouse) — the lower-effort version.
Just Schatzkammer (~45 min + coffeehouse) — the lightest version.

Evening

Backup options if Hofburg/Treasury falls through

In rough order of strength for this trip:

  • Secession Building + Klimt's Beethoven Frieze — 2 min walk from Naschmarkt's north end. The 1898 art-nouveau exhibition hall with the golden "cabbage" dome. In the basement: Klimt's permanently installed 34-meter Beethoven Frieze (1902). ~30 min focused. Pairs with Friday's Belvedere Klimt.
  • Mozarthaus + Imperial Crypt — 60 min Mozarthaus (his only surviving Vienna apartment, behind Stephansdom) + 45 min Imperial Crypt (Habsburg burial vault on Neuer Markt, 145 imperial bodies including Sisi). Same neighbourhood, both small.
  • Hundertwasserhaus + Kunst Haus Wien — walking distance from the apartment. Colourful expressionist Hundertwasser's residential building plus a museum of his work three blocks away. ~90 min combined.
  • Fiaker carriage ride — 40-min horse-drawn carriage loop through the Innere Stadt. €80 per carriage. Mobility-perfect.
  • Vienna State Opera tour + Haus der Musik — 40-min Opera tour + 90-min interactive music museum.
