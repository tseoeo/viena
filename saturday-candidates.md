## Morning: Naschmarkt + Flohmarkt (locked)

A 1.5-km open-air market between Karlsplatz (north) and Kettenbrückengasse (south), running since the 16th century. ~120 permanent food stalls plus ~20 restaurants spilling into the path. Saturday only: the **Flohmarkt** (flea market) sets up at the southern end — ~400 vendors, antiques, vintage clothing, vinyl, Habsburg-era postcards, Turkish/Persian textiles, junk piles to sift through.

**Suggested flow** (~3–3.5 hours total):
1. **9:00** leave apartment, U4 from Wien Mitte (or Stadtpark) to **Kettenbrückengasse** (south end) — 3 stops, ~6 min.
2. **9:30–11:00** Flohmarkt while energy is fresh and selection is best. Bring cash (~€100–200), wallet in front pocket.
3. **11:00–11:30** slow walk north into the food market — taste cheese, olives, smell the spice stalls, watch fishmongers.
4. **11:30–12:30** sit-down brunch at one of the Naschmarkt restaurants:
   - **Naschmarkt Deli** — popular sit-down spot, large breakfast plates, Viennese-meets-Levantine
   - **Neni am Naschmarkt** — Israeli/Levantine fusion (the Sabich is famous)
   - **Café Restaurant Drechsler** (south end) — calm classic Vienna café with Naschmarkt twist
   - **Umar Fisch** — fish/oysters lunch
5. **~12:30–13:00** end at Karlsplatz, U4 back to apartment area for a brief reset.

**What's worth taking home**: small Augarten porcelain piece (real ones marked "AW") from a flea market dealer, Styrian pumpkin seed oil from a cheese stall, Manner wafers, an old Vienna print or postcard.

**If it rains**: flea market mostly packs up; food market is partially covered, restaurants are indoor. Switch to a long Naschmarkt food-market brunch only.

## Afternoon: Hofburg short-form + Imperial Treasury (leaning)

The Hofburg complex is the Habsburgs' city palace and the seat of imperial power for over 600 years. Two ticketed pieces are the meaningful afternoon options — they sit inside the same complex but require separate tickets.

### Hofburg short-form: Sisi Museum + Imperial Apartments + Silver Collection (one combined ticket)

~90 min. The "Habsburgs at home" experience that Belvedere doesn't give you.

- **Sisi Museum** — the story of Empress Elisabeth: her unhappy royal marriage, beauty rituals, exercise obsession, wandering, assassination by an anarchist in Geneva 1898.
- **Imperial Apartments** — the 19 rooms where Franz Joseph and Sisi actually lived: bedrooms, audience chamber, his austere iron camp-bed (he slept on it for 50 years), her exercise room, dining table set for state guests.
- **Silver Collection** — imperial tableware, including a 30-meter-long baroque silver centerpiece for state banquets, gold-plated services for 140 guests.

**Pre-book** timed entry on sisimuseum-hofburg.at. Synthesis-validated open on May 2 (Saturday is a normal day; not the May 1 issue).

### Imperial Treasury (Schatzkammer)

~45 min. Separate ticket from Sisi/Apartments, in a different wing of the Hofburg.

One of the great treasure rooms of Europe. Headlines:

- **Crown of the Holy Roman Empire** (10th century, oldest crown in Europe)
- **Imperial regalia** — orb, scepter, ceremonial sword
- **Crown of the Austrian Empire**
- **The Burgundian inheritance** — treasures from the 1477 marriage of Maximilian I to Mary of Burgundy
- **One of the world's largest emeralds** — a baroque perfume jar, 2,860 carats, carved from a single emerald
- **Habsburg jewels and personal collection** spanning centuries
- **Religious relics and the "narwhal horn"** kept as a unicorn-horn relic (one of the strangest items in any treasury)

If mom is a "this craftsmanship is unbelievable" person, this is unmissable. If she's more focused on the human/imperial-life side, the Sisi/Apartments piece is the priority.

### How to combine

- **Both** (the "imperial double", ~3 hours): Sisi + Apartments first while energy is fresh, brief sit-down break, then walk across the Hofburg complex to the Schatzkammer. Coffeehouse on the way back.
- **Just Sisi + Apartments** (~90 min + coffeehouse): the lower-effort version. Gives the imperial-life dose without the additional museum.
- **Just Schatzkammer** (~45 min + coffeehouse): the lightest version. Just the headline treasure room.

**Decide based on energy after the Naschmarkt morning.** If the morning was lively but tiring, do just Sisi/Apartments. If you have legs left, add the Treasury.

### Coffeehouse near Hofburg

- **Café Hawelka** (Dorotheergasse 6, ~5 min walk) — small, dim, bohemian, the post-WWII artists' café. Famous Buchteln (jam-filled yeast pastries) baked in the late afternoon.
- **Café Landtmann** (on the Ring opposite the Burgtheater, ~10 min walk) — grand, formal, Sigmund Freud's regular café. Easier with elderly mobility.

## Backup options (if Hofburg/Treasury falls through or doesn't fit on the day)

In rough order of strength for this trip:

1. **Secession Building + Klimt's Beethoven Frieze** — *2 min walk from Naschmarkt's north end.* The 1898 art-nouveau exhibition hall with the golden "cabbage" dome. In the basement: Klimt's permanently installed 34-meter Beethoven Frieze (1902). ~30 min focused. Pairs with Friday's Belvedere Klimt.
2. **Mozarthaus + Imperial Crypt** — 60 min Mozarthaus (his only surviving Vienna apartment, behind Stephansdom) + 45 min Imperial Crypt (Habsburg burial vault on Neuer Markt, 145 imperial bodies including Sisi). Same neighborhood, both small.
3. **Hundertwasserhaus + Kunst Haus Wien** — *walking distance from your apartment.* Colorful expressionist Hundertwasser's residential building plus a museum of his work three blocks away. ~90 min combined.
4. **Fiaker carriage ride** — 40-min horse-drawn carriage loop through the Innere Stadt. Stands at Stephansplatz, Albertinaplatz, Heldenplatz. €80 per carriage. Mobility-perfect.
5. **Vienna State Opera tour + Haus der Musik** — 40-min Opera tour + 90-min interactive music museum. Closes the trip's musical arc.
