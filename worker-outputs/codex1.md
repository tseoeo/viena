## Key Findings

1. > "All areas are accessible by elevator." "Seating accommodations are available in the exhibition galleries." "The pathway through the park connecting the Upper and Lower Belvedere is not barrier-free."
Source: https://www.belvedere.at/en/your-visit/inclusion-museum
Belvedere is the best fit for this exact trip if the hotel is nearby and the goal is an imperial-feeling visit with controlled walking. The buildings themselves are elder-friendly by museum standards, but the famous sloped garden axis is the weak point, so the practical move is to treat Upper Belvedere as the core and not assume a leisurely up-and-down garden traversal.

2. > "The visit to the Upper and Lower Belvedere is only possible with a valid time slot ticket." "You choose your time slot from a list of times available..." "The duration of your stay, however, is unlimited." "It is busiest between 11 am and 2 pm."
Sources: https://www.belvedere.at/en/visit ; https://www.belvedere.at/en/faq ; https://www.belvedere.at/en/house-rules
Belvedere should be pre-booked. It is the easiest imperial sight here to turn into a low-stress 60-120 minute outing: book an early Upper Belvedere slot, avoid the 11:00-14:00 crowd window, and rest inside as needed because there is no forced exit time once admitted.

3. > "Schönbrunn Palace is open daily, including public holidays!" "Tip: Pre-book an online ticket." "The Palace ... can be visited without barriers by wheelchair users." "The direct access to the garden via a ramp is located directly to the right of the palace..." "The Privy Garden and Maze viewing platform, the Privy Garden arcade and the Gloriette viewing terrace are only accessible via stairs."
Sources: https://www.schoenbrunn.at/en/visitor-information/opening-times ; https://www.schoenbrunn.at/en/visitor-information/faq ; https://www.schoenbrunn.at/en/visitor-information/barrier-free-access/
Schönbrunn is operationally open and bookable even on Thursday, 1 May 2026, but it is not the obvious elderly-friendly choice unless the family strongly wants the grand summer-residence experience. The core palace is accessible, yet the signature add-on outdoor features are exactly where the stair burden returns.

4. > "Discover the State Apartments ... gain a compact insight..." "Duration c. 40 mins" "Palace Ticket ... Duration c. 75 mins" "Classic Pass ... Duration approx. 1-2 days" / "Duration 3-4 hours."
Source: https://www.schoenbrunn.at/en/tickets-and-prices/all-tickets-tours
Schönbrunn is only worth it for this trip in its short form, not as a full imperial campus day. If chosen, the strongest elderly-parent version is the 40-minute State Apartments ticket, not the 75-minute Palace Ticket and definitely not the garden-heavy combined passes that expand the visit into hours or even multiple days.

5. > "All exhibition areas are accessible via ramps, mobile wheelchair rails and via elevator..." "There are a number of chairs in the state rooms." "Be sure to secure your admission time in advance by purchasing an online ticket." "We can only guarantee admission with an online ticket for a booked time slot."
Sources: https://www.sisimuseum-hofburg.at/en/visitor-information/barrier-free-access/ ; https://www.sisimuseum-hofburg.at/en/visitor-information/faq
The best 90-minute Hofburg version is a very short-form Hofburg: pre-book the Sisi Museum + Imperial Apartments and do not try to "cover Hofburg" as a whole. This is the most realistic way to get the lived-in Habsburg interior experience without turning the day into a long complex-to-complex museum march.

6. > "Duration ca. 60 minutes" "Daily and on public holidays | 9 am - 5.30 pm" "It is possible to stay in the Sisi Museum until 5.00 pm and at the Imperial Apartments until 5.30 pm." "From January 29, 2026, access to the Sisi Museum will change temporarily. Access with valid tickets will be via the Kaisertor."
Sources: https://www.sisimuseum-hofburg.at/en/ ; https://www.sisimuseum-hofburg.at/en/visitor-information/opening-hours ; https://www.sisimuseum-hofburg.at/en/visitor-information/directions/?cHash=0a30fe6b675eb58b486666619c657245&chash=eb2f0e0476a57b57abd7749beee60bc8
Practically, Hofburg is well-shaped for a 60-90 minute visit and is open on public holidays, which helps answer the May 1 concern on the imperial-sights side. The only operational wrinkle I found is the temporary 2026 access change via the Kaisertor, so this is one place where following the exact booked-entry instructions matters.

7. > "The silver collection will remain closed from April 1, 2023 until further notice." "No. The Imperial Treasury Vienna is also in the Hofburg... Regrettably not at the current time."
Source: https://www.sisimuseum-hofburg.at/en/visitor-information/faq
Hofburg is actually narrower than many visitors assume right now: the Silver Collection is closed, there is no all-in-one Hofburg combo ticket with Treasury or Spanish Riding School, and the official site itself does not support a broad all-Hofburg sweep. That makes the case for a compact Imperial Apartments/Sisi Museum visit even stronger.

8. > "With its inviting lawn, views of magnificent imperial architecture..." "Curved paths meander past people, forming a circular route..." "The terrace of Brasserie Palmenhaus..." "01 April to 31 October ... daily, 06:00 - 22:00."
Sources: https://www.wien.info/en/livable-vienna/parks-green-spaces/burggarten-994538 ; https://www.bundesgaerten.at/hofburggaerten/oeffnungszeiten0.html
If the family wants an imperial garden atmosphere without committing to Schönbrunn, Burggarten is the best related add-on: central, open long hours in May, visually tied to Hofburg, and useful as a seated decompression stop with nearby café infrastructure. It is not a substitute for palace interiors, but it is a better low-effort garden companion to Hofburg than adding Schönbrunn just for "more gardens."

## Surprising or Contradictory

- Belvedere's interiors are the easiest part, while the iconic garden link between Upper and Lower Belvedere is explicitly not barrier-free. The postcard view is real, but the practical elderly-parent version is more limited than the image suggests.
- Schönbrunn looks broadly accessible in official language, yet several marquee outdoor features remain stair-only. In other words, "barrier-free palace" and "easy garden day" are not the same thing here.
- Hofburg is less sprawling in practice than in reputation: the Silver Collection is closed, there is no grand Hofburg mega-ticket, and the official product most suited to ordinary visitors is already a roughly 60-minute fixed-time visit.
- The May 1 concern is less problematic for these imperial sights than expected: Schönbrunn explicitly says it is open daily including public holidays, and the Sisi Museum opening-hours page also says daily and on public holidays.
